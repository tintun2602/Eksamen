The Problem

Recycling might seem like a boring problem, don’t it? Our garbage might not seem like gold. At least not in first sight. What’s important to realise is that many of the things we throw away on a daily basis still have enormous value in terms of their resources. Things can be used and reused multiple times. Whether it be cotton, plastic or metals. The EPA estimates that 75% of all waste is recyclable, but in the US only 30 % of that is actually recycled. We in RESET want to do something about this problem. Join us in this adventure!

Why is recycling important?

Recycling is the process of converting waste materials into new materials and objects. The recyclabiltiy of a material depends on its ability to reaquire the properties it had in its virgin or original state. It is an alternative to "conventional" waste disposal that can save material and help lower greenhouse gas emissions. Recycling can prevent the waste of potentially useful materials and reduce the consumption and reduce the consumption of fresh raw materials, thereby reducing: energy usage, air pollution and water poluttion.
