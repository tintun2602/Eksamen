Problem

Today we live in an era of information overload. With the help of the internet, informaton circulates around the globe at incredible rates, and in present times the average human takes in five times as much information daily, compared to 1986. We see how it can be seen as ironic to now serve you more statistics, facts and figures. Even scientists have a touch of humor, haha! However, such easy and quick access to information quickly leads to the spread of false information, so it is important to be critical and cautious over the information we read. That is why we want to inform you rightly about central topics in the climate crisis and this debate; the garbage debate.

Recycling around the world

Many countries do not have the necessary and proper infrastructure and culture for recycling and handling of waste. Instead of refining and establishing change in foreign infrastructures, we in re-sort wanted to solve this problem with a more reasonable and realistic method. After all, people follow the road of least resistance. Therefore we wanted our solution to work for and around the existing patterns of behaviour. With our solution all of the difficulties of recycling gets done without the user having to think about it at all.

Economic benefits of recycling

By reusing materials and resources we can produce far more equipment and products for more people. With the growing population, and many studies estimate that we will reach a population count of 11 billion people by 2100, it makes sense to use our natural resources more sparingly. Many companies we look up to use materials in clever ways. One inspiring company is EVERFUEL, which works with the conversion of plastics to fuel.

Positive effects of recycling

If everyone consumed as much as the average Norwegian we would need almost four globes to meet the demand. With recycling we can produce and deliver more goods to more people. If we choose this perspective recycling can be viewed as a charitable act, not only for ourselves but for our whole global community.

UN Sustainable Development Goal #13

The 13.th Sustainable Development Goal is to take urgent action to combat climate change and its impacts. Global temperatures are projected to rise up to 3.2 celsius by 2100. The United Nations goal is to work towards reducing this number to 1.5 celsius. We were very much inspired by this goal when we set up our initial mission.
