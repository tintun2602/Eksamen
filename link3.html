The Consequences

“That which we need the most will be found where we least want to look.” ~ Carl Jung.
Much can be said about climate change, and the negative effects our behaviours have on the planet.
People are still to this day unsure if these things have real impact, or if it mostly is a hype.
In this section we want to look closer to those assumptions.

Polluted ocean

Pollution at our local beach often annoys us. But did you know that only 6 % of the plastic pollution in the sea is visible from the surface? Tons and tons of plastic sink to the bottom of the ocean, leading to devastating effects on both animal and plants. 15 tonns of plastic enters the ocean every minute. Many studies shows that this number could increase to 60 tonns a minute by 2050. This also effects us very directly, through micro-plastics. Did you know people on average consume 10 grams of plastic a week due to this piling up in our food?

Global warming

Global warming; We all have heard about the consequences of global warming. Most are also familiar with the rising sea levels. But why is all of this happening? And what could the consequences be? With the excess burning of carbon we increase the amount of co2 in the atmosphere. This traps heat and energy. With the increase of temperatures many places will not longer be livable. With the higher sea levels natural disasters will follow. Places will become unstable and people will be left without their homes

Forest fire

Did you know that your waste contributes to global warming thereby accelerates the process of forest fire? Some of the waste which is thrown into communal trash bins are not sorted, therefore there are tons of waste which is not recycled due to malpractice. Some waste will eventually rot, but that is not the case for all. As a consequence, some of the waste will generate methane gas, which is in fact explosive. This is a major contributor to the greenhouse effect which is the main factor for the rise of forest fire seen in recent years.

Endager of animals

Every single day, animals around the world face the potential of being extinct due to waste which is laying around everywhere. Animals are easily attracted to discarded food boards, water cups etc. left by people. As some seasons can be extremely harsh for animals, thus facing starvation they are left with no other option then to eat remains. Among the worst form of waste is plastic which polystyrene foam. This type of plastic never fully vanishes due to its degradation time. This type of waste will haunt the wild life and endanger animals for centuries to come.
