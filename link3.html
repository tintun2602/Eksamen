The Consequences

“That which we need the most will be found where we least want to look.” ~ Carl Jung. Much can be said about climate change, and the negative effects our behaviours have on the planet. People are still to this day unsure if these things have a real impact on our planet, or if it mostly false information and lies. In this section we want to look closer to these assumptions.

Polluted ocean

Pollution at our local beach often annoys us. But did you know that only 6 % of the plastic pollution in the sea is visible from the surface? Countless tons of plastic has sunk to the bottom of the ocean throughout the last couple of decades, leading to devastating effects on both animal and plants. 15 tons of plastic enters the ocean every minute. Many studies shows that this number could increase to 60 tonns a minute by 2050. This also effects us very directly, through micro-plastics. Did you know people on average consume 10 grams of plastic a week due to this piling up in our food?

Global warming

Global warming; We all have heard about the consequences of global warming. Most are also familiar with the rising sea levels. But why is all of this happening? And what could the consequences be? With the excess burning of carbon we increase the amount of co2 in the atmosphere. This traps heat and energy, and slowly increases the global temperature within our atmosphere. This increase of temperature is the main cause for why the ice caps in both the north- and south- pole are melting, and these large masses of ice significantly increase the water levels when melter. Only a few meters of water level elevation is enough to flood giant areas, making them unhospitable for humans and animals alike. Higher water levels quickly lead to an increased rate of natural disasters, which already in present times are devastatiing in countries of the third world.

Forest fire

Did you know that your waste contributes to global warming thereby accelerates the process of forest fire? Some of the waste which is thrown into communal trash bins are not sorted, therefore there are tons of waste which is not recycled due to malpractice. Some waste will eventually rot, but that is not the case for all. Plastics for example, take countless years to be broken down, and even then the plastic particles are harmful for us organisms. As a consequence, some of the waste will generate methane gas, which is in fact explosive. This is a major contributor to the greenhouse effect which is the main factor for the rise of forest fire seen in recent years.

Endagerment of wild-life

Every single day, animals around the world face extinction due to the wastes dumped in their natural habitats. A lot of the trash left in such habitats have toxic properties, harmful for the animals which feed upon those remains. Polystyrene foam is among one of the most harmful and common materials animals in the wild life are exponsed to. This type of plastic never fully vanishes due to its long degradation time, unlike organic materials. The remains of such materials can remain within organism for years, if not centuries, traveling throughout the food chain.
